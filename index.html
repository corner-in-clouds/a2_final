Are accidents frequently related to alcohol?





When combined with alcohol, skill and decision-making ability are reduced, while confidence and aggression are increased. There is a possibility that it will lead to an increase in other risky behaviour. According to studies, every 0.05 increase in blood alcohol content (BAC) above zero doubles the likelihood of being involved in a fatal car accident.


Because not all fatal and serious crash drivers are subjected to blood alcohol content and drug testing, this fact sheet only includes those who have been tested and whose results have been made publicly available. Therefore, some crashes in which no alcohol or drugs were involved may have been caused by alcohol or drugs in some cases, and vice versa.

Number of accidents under different light conditions...


I will be mainly displaying the proportions of accidents under dark conditions, but here is a graph between day and dark:

At night, car accident statistics are startling. Despite the fact that there is 60% less traffic on the roads at night, more than 40% of all fatal car accidents occur at night. Sunlight is the most powerful light source. When the sun goes down, there are a number of man-made lights that assist cars in securely navigating the highways. There is a graph demonstrating the proportion of accidents not in days.

Driving at night is always more risky, regardless of whether the road is familiar or not. According to Injury Facts, almost 40,000 individuals were killed in car accidents in 2016.

Analysis related to Speed zone


In Victoria's roads, speed is one of the most significant factors contributing to accidents, and research has shown that even small reductions in speed can result in a significant reduction in road trauma. In normal driving conditions, it will take a car travelling at 60 km/h 45 minutes to come to a complete stop in an emergency braking situation. Despite slowing down from 65km/h, a car travelling at close to 32km/h after 45m will still be moving at close to that speed.
